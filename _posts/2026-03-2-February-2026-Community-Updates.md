<b>Hong Kong is all-in on AI</b>

Despite macro volatility, AI remains to be the focus down to regional level. Coming back to Hong Kong, where OAX foundation is headquartered, we’ve been to Consensus early this month as well as an investor network dinner hosted by Cyberport, we’re seeing increased strategic allocation into the cities’ AI development both from private and public sector. 

Hong Kong's 2026-27 Budget, delivered by Financial Secretary Paul Chan on February 25, 2026, under the theme "Driving High-quality, Inclusive Growth with Innovation and Finance," positioned artificial intelligence (AI) as the central pillar of its economic strategy to foster high-quality, inclusive development amid sustained economic momentum. 

Key AI-focused initiatives include establishing and personally chairing the <b>Committee on AI+</b> and Industry Development Strategy to formulate policies promoting "AI for industries" and "industries for AI," with initial emphasis on life and health technology, embodied AI, banking applications, and broader societal adoption; launching an AI+ strategy to accelerate industrial transformation; allocating HK$50 million (~US$6.4 million) to enhance AI literacy through university courses, workforce training via a new Skills Upgrading Board.

The Hong Kong Artificial Intelligence Research and Development Institute in the second half of 2026 to drive R&D, application conversion, and government advisory; continuing the HK$3 billion AI Subsidy Scheme, which has already approved around 30 R&D projects in areas like large language models, new materials, and biomedicine; and supporting trials in financial services through sandboxes (e.g., HKMA and <a href="https://x.com/OAX_Foundation/status/1987788132444741924">Cyberport</a>) alongside cross-border efforts such as an MOU with Guangxi for AI applications and data collaboration. 

These measures aim to industrialize AI, deepen its integration across sectors, popularize its use society-wide, and leverage Hong Kong's strengths in finance and innovation to align with national priorities and attract talent and investment

<b>GMAsia Campus AI: The Convergence</b>

Since Q4 2025, OAX Foundation has begun supporting <a href="https://gmasia.ai/">GMAsia</a>. They are at the forefront of addressing Asia's accelerating <b>AI boom</b> and the stark disparities in <b>AI literacy</b>, aligning closely with forward-thinking government strategies, like Hong Kong's recent budget allocation for universal AI training to build practical skills and literacy across society. 

Through <b>GMAsia Campus</b>, this project is bridging the gap for individuals (builders, students, professionals) and institutions alike by creating a leading AI EdTech platform that connects top thought leaders with eager communities via curated events, comprehensive courses, ethical frameworks, and collaborative ecosystems. Fresh momentum from recent hackathons has sparked strong interest in involvement, surfacing synergies and partnership opportunities that position GMAsia Campus as the go-to hub for upskilling, responsible AI adoption, and unlocking innovation.

Hence one of the most exciting outcomes connecting the dots for <b>OAX Foundation</b> over the past year is <b>GMAsia</b>. The clear manifestation of our push into <b>AI x Web3</b> in Hong Kong. From launching the GMAsia events hub in late 2025, to deep involvement at <b>Consensus Hong Kong 2026</b> it all converged into <b>GMAsia</b>.

<b>OAX x HackTheEast AI Hackathon</b>

We’re pleased to see Hong Kong’s government strategic shift in AI begins to converge with the foundation’s effort in AI research and developments. Hence this came timely with our AI focused hackathon with students across the region.

OAX sponsored <b>HackTheEast</b>, the organiser of Hong Kong's first largest student-led AI hackathon, culminated over the weekend with outstanding execution and innovation. The event drew over 600 applications, narrowing to 60+ exceptional projects that delivered compelling elevator pitches at HKUST. We were truly impressed by what teams built in just 24 hours, tackling real-world challenges in EdTech, FinTech, privacy blockchain, and PropTech while showcasing strong coding skills, presentation prowess, impact, and teamwork. 

OAX Foundation proudly participated in judging the main prize and presented our dedicated OAX side challenge award in education development. What began with a few phone calls and a shared vision to empower the next generation of innovators, unlocking students' potential to create with AI, free from corporate red tape has grown into something special.

Huge congrats to the winners, participants and the organising committee.
